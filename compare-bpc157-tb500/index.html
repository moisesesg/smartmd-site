Key differences in preclinical research.

BPC-157 is a 15-amino-acid fragment derived from gastric juice, studied primarily in preclinical models of musculoskeletal tissue signaling (tendon-to-bone healing, ligament repair) and gut epithelium protection. It's notably stable in acidic environments. TB-500 is a 43-amino-acid synthetic fragment of Thymosin Beta 4, a naturally occurring peptide found in virtually all human cells. TB-500 is studied in preclinical models of cell migration, wound repair, cardiac tissue signaling, and systemic anti-inflammatory pathways.

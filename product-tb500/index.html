What is TB-500?

TB-500 is a synthetic fragment of Thymosin Beta 4 (TB4), a naturally occurring 43-amino-acid peptide found in virtually every human and animal cell. Thymosin Beta 4 plays a critical role in actin sequestration, cell migration, and tissue repair signaling. TB-500 replicates the active region of the full-length protein for targeted research applications.

With a molecular weight of approximately 4,963 Da, TB-500 has been the subject of 50+ preclinical studies examining its role in wound repair, tissue regeneration, anti-inflammatory signaling, cardiac tissue models, and hair follicle signaling pathways.

TB-500 is classified as a research-use-only compound. It is not FDA-approved for any therapeutic indication. All references below describe preclinical (in vitro or animal model) research contexts, not human clinical outcomes.

Published preclinical research areas

Cell migration & wound repair

TB-500 is studied for its role in promoting cell migration to sites of injury. Research examines its effects on keratinocyte and endothelial cell movement, critical steps in wound closure and vascular repair models.

Tissue regeneration

Preclinical literature examines TB-500 in models of muscle, tendon, and ligament tissue regeneration. Its actin-binding properties are hypothesized to facilitate cytoskeletal reorganization during tissue repair processes.

Anti-inflammatory signaling

Published research explores TB-500's effects on inflammatory cytokine modulation and NF-kB pathway regulation. Studies suggest a role in reducing inflammatory responses in tissue injury models.

Cardiac & hair follicle models

Emerging research examines TB-500 in cardiac tissue repair models following ischemic events, as well as hair follicle stem cell activation and anagen phase signaling in dermatological research contexts.

Why the old suppliers disappeared.

It wasn't one thing. It was a regulatory wave.

FDA enforcement targeted direct-to-consumer

Suppliers making therapeutic claims, selling without research-use labeling, or operating outside compliance frameworks got hit. The common denominator was marketing peptides as if they were approved medicines — that's the line the FDA drew.

Payment processors cut off gray-market vendors

Stripe, PayPal, and bank payment rails pulled support for suppliers flagged as compliance risks. Many couldn't accept payments, couldn't refund customers, couldn't fulfill pending orders. Thousands of researchers lost orders + store credit.

State boards added pressure on practitioners

State medical boards and pharmacy boards issued advisories about sourcing. Clinicians sourcing from flagged suppliers faced licensing scrutiny. The risk calculus changed overnight for any practice using gray-market supply.

Single-source dependency collapsed the system

Most clinics + researchers had one supplier. When that supplier went dark, there was no backup. The lesson: verified secondary-source relationships are now the standard. We can be that.

What is SS-31?

SS-31 (Elamipretide), also known as a Szeto-Schiller peptide, is a cell-permeable tetrapeptide (D-Arg-Dmt-Lys-Phe-NH2) that selectively targets the inner mitochondrial membrane. It is the mitochondria peptide — targeting the inner mitochondrial membrane directly, unlike any other compound class in current research.

With 50+ preclinical publications, SS-31 has been studied extensively in models of mitochondrial inner membrane stabilization, cardiolipin binding, oxidative stress reduction, cellular energy restoration, and aging-related mitochondrial dysfunction. Its unique mechanism involves direct binding to cardiolipin on the inner mitochondrial membrane, stabilizing electron transport chain function.

SS-31 is classified as a research-use-only compound. It is not FDA-approved for any therapeutic indication. All references below describe preclinical (in vitro or animal model) research contexts, not human clinical outcomes.

Published preclinical research areas

Mitochondrial inner membrane stabilization

Studied in preclinical models examining direct binding to cardiolipin in the inner mitochondrial membrane. Research explores stabilization of cristae structure, electron transport chain efficiency, and prevention of cytochrome c release.

Cardiolipin binding

Preclinical studies examine SS-31's selective interaction with cardiolipin — a phospholipid unique to the inner mitochondrial membrane. Research explores how this binding prevents cardiolipin peroxidation and maintains membrane potential.

Oxidative stress reduction

Research examines SS-31's role in reducing mitochondrial reactive oxygen species (ROS) production at the source — by optimizing electron transport chain function rather than scavenging free radicals after they are produced.

Cellular energy and aging models

Preclinical research explores SS-31 in age-related mitochondrial dysfunction, ATP production restoration, skeletal muscle energetics, cardiac function models, and neurodegenerative disease models involving mitochondrial impairment.

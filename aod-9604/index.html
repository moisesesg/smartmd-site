What is AOD-9604?

AOD-9604 (Anti-Obesity Drug 9604) is a modified fragment of human growth hormone corresponding to amino acids 176-191 of the hGH polypeptide. It was specifically engineered to isolate the fat-metabolizing region of growth hormone while excluding the growth-promoting effects associated with full-length hGH.

The fat-specific GH fragment demonstrates lipolytic activity without growth-promoting effects in preclinical models. With 30+ publications, AOD-9604 has been studied in fat metabolism signaling, lipolytic pathway activation, and cartilage repair models.

AOD-9604 is classified as a research-use-only compound. It is not FDA-approved for any therapeutic indication. All references below describe preclinical (in vitro or animal model) research contexts, not human clinical outcomes.

Published preclinical research areas

Fat metabolism signaling

Studied in preclinical models examining adipose tissue lipolysis activation. Research explores beta-3 adrenergic receptor interaction and fat oxidation pathways independent of IGF-1 elevation.

Lipolytic activity without growth effects

A defining characteristic of AOD-9604 — preclinical studies demonstrate fat-reducing activity without the diabetogenic, growth-promoting, or IGF-1-elevating effects of full-length GH. This separation of effects is a key research focus.

Cartilage repair models

Emerging research examines AOD-9604's role in chondrocyte proliferation and cartilage matrix synthesis models, suggesting potential applications beyond fat metabolism in musculoskeletal research.

Metabolic pathway modulation

Preclinical studies explore AOD-9604's interactions with metabolic signaling cascades including glucose uptake modulation and energy expenditure pathways in adipose tissue models.

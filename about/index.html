The SMART MD Standard

7 Steps. No Shortcuts.

Every compound goes through this process before it's available. No batches skip steps. No exceptions based on volume, timeline, or supplier relationship.

01

GMP Synthesis Sourcing

Every peptide originates from a verified synthesis partner operating under Good Manufacturing Practice conditions with documented protocols and chain-of-custody records.

02

Internal QC Gate

Visual inspection, lyophilization quality assessment, packaging integrity check, and preliminary identity screening. Batches that don't clear this gate never reach external labs.

03

HPLC Analysis — Lab 1

High-performance liquid chromatography at an independent, accredited laboratory. Confirms peptide identity and purity percentage. Any result below 98.0% is a hard reject — no exceptions, no appeals.

✓ Minimum threshold: 98.0% purity
04

Mass Spectrometry — Lab 2

Independent mass spectrometry at a second facility. Confirms molecular weight matches the target compound and rules out structural analogues or truncated sequences that HPLC alone cannot detect.

05

Endotoxin & Microbial — Lab 3

LAL endotoxin assay and full microbial contamination panel at a third independent facility. Standard practice in pharmaceutical-grade manufacturing. The only way to confirm bioburden — HPLC doesn't tell you this.

✓ Must pass <5 EU/mg threshold
06

COA Compilation & Batch Logging

Certificate of Analysis compiled from all three lab reports. Batch number assigned and logged to our public verification system. Permanently searchable at smartmdpeptides.com/verify.

07

Released to Researchers

Only after all three labs pass their respective thresholds does a batch become available. This adds time to the supply chain. We've explicitly decided that's the correct tradeoff for researchers who depend on accurate data.

✓ COA verifiable at any time by any researcher
The Founder

Moe

From championship athletics to research-grade compounds — built from personal experience, not a business plan.

Moe represented the United States at the Junior Taekwondo Olympics. He was an MMA Champion across multiple disciplines simultaneously — wrestling, striking, grappling. He competed in wrestling, track, and cross country at a level where most people only talk about one sport. He didn't pick a lane. He ran all of them.

Then came the injury. A lower back injury that didn't just end a training cycle — it changed how he lived. For years, he could only sleep on his back. Recovery wasn't optional. It was survival.

That experience pushed him into healthcare and medical sales — intentionally. Not because it was the obvious move, but because he refused to stand behind products that didn't meet his standard. He'd been the person on the other side of the research. He knew what it felt like when the compound didn't do what the label claimed.

When he discovered peptide research, the science was unlike anything he'd seen. The data was compelling. The potential was real. But the market was a mess — unreliable suppliers, self-reported purity, COAs that couldn't be traced. The people who needed this research the most had no way to trust what they were buying.

So he built SMART MD. Not as a business idea — as a mission. Three independent U.S. labs verify every batch. No self-reported data. No shortcuts. The standard he wished existed when he was the one searching for answers.

Today, 5,000+ researchers trust what he built from personal pain. SMART MD Peptides isn't a brand that was designed in a boardroom. It was built by someone who needed it to exist.

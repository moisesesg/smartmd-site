AOD 9604 — the lipolytic GH fragment

AOD 9604 is a modified fragment of the C-terminus of human growth hormone (residues 176-191) with an N-terminal tyrosine addition. Research has examined its ability to stimulate lipolysis and inhibit lipogenesis in adipose tissue models — without activating the somatotropic pathway engaged by full-length GH.

  • Lipolysis without GH-like effects — separates lipolytic activity from growth/IGF-1 pathway
  • Adipocyte research — studied in rodent and in vitro adipocyte models
  • Short peptide stability — smaller than native GH, different pharmacokinetics

5-Amino-1MQ — the NNMT inhibitor

5-Amino-1MQ is a small molecule inhibitor of nicotinamide N-methyltransferase (NNMT). NNMT transfers methyl groups from SAM (S-adenosylmethionine) to nicotinamide. Research has shown that NNMT is elevated in obese adipose tissue in mouse models, and inhibition reduces adipose mass and improves metabolic parameters in those models.

  • NNMT inhibition — restores methyl group availability for SAM-dependent methylations
  • Adipose tissue — preclinical studies show reduced adipose mass with chronic administration
  • Metabolic parameters — glucose, insulin, lipid markers in high-fat-diet mouse models
  • Orally active — tablet formulation available

MOTS-c — mitochondrial-derived peptide

MOTS-c (Mitochondrial Open Reading frame of the 12S rRNA-c) is a 16-amino-acid peptide encoded by the mitochondrial genome. Research has examined its role in metabolic homeostasis — it communicates mitochondrial status to the nucleus, modulating AMPK and insulin sensitivity pathways in preclinical models.

  • Mitochondrial-encoded peptide — unusual origin; most peptides are nuclear-encoded
  • AMPK pathway — activates cellular energy-sensing pathway
  • Insulin sensitivity — studied in high-fat-diet mouse models
  • Exercise physiology — exercise-induced changes in circulating MOTS-c are an active research area

Cagrilintide — amylin analogue

Cagrilintide is a long-acting amylin analogue. Amylin is co-secreted with insulin from pancreatic beta cells and contributes to satiety signaling. Research has examined Cagrilintide especially in combination with GLP-1 agonists (Cagri+Sema) where the combined amylin + GLP-1 pathway engagement produces larger adiposity effects than either alone in preclinical models.

  • Amylin receptor engagement — complementary to GLP-1 pathway
  • Long half-life — extended-duration amylin analogue
  • Combination research — Cagri + Sema (CagriSema) is a major emerging research pairing
  • Satiety signaling — central effects on food intake regulation in rodent models

Frequently asked questions

Full-length human GH activates both the lipolytic pathway (fat breakdown) and the somatotropic pathway (growth, IGF-1). AOD 9604 is a modified fragment designed to isolate the lipolytic activity without the growth/IGF-1 effects. This separation is why AOD 9604 is studied specifically in adiposity contexts rather than growth contexts.

Technically 5-Amino-1MQ is a small molecule (not a peptide) — it's a quinoline derivative that inhibits the NNMT enzyme. It is included in peptide research catalogs because it targets the same metabolic research space as peptide compounds and is studied in similar preclinical models of obesity and metabolic dysfunction.

Amylin (Cagrilintide) and GLP-1 (GLP-1SM) engage different satiety pathways. Combined, they produce larger magnitude effects on food intake and body weight in preclinical comparison studies than either compound alone. This has made CagriSema one of the most-studied emerging research combinations in obesity literature.

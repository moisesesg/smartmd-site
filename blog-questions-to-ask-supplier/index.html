# 10 Questions to Ask Before Choosing a Research Peptide Supplier

---

The fastest way to judge a research peptide supplier is to ask the right questions and listen carefully to the answers. A quality supplier responds directly and specifically. A weak one deflects or gives vague reassurances. Here are ten questions worth asking before you order - each with the technical reason it matters.

1. Do you provide a batch-specific Certificate of Analysis for every product?

Why it matters: The COA is your evidence of what is in the vial. It must be tied to the exact lot you receive - because every batch is a fresh synthesis. "Yes, batch-specific" is the answer you want.

2. Who performs your testing - is it an independent lab?

Why it matters: Independent, third-party testing removes the supplier's conflict of interest. An outside lab has no stake in whether a batch passes. In-house-only testing is far weaker evidence.

3. Does your COA include mass spectrometry, not just HPLC?

Why it matters: HPLC measures purity but cannot confirm identity. A peptide missing one amino acid can read 99% pure yet be the wrong compound. Mass spectrometry confirms the molecular weight - and therefore the identity. You want both.

4. What purity standard do you guarantee?

Why it matters: A real supplier states a clear, documented standard. For most research use, 98% by HPLC is the accepted research-grade benchmark; 99% is a premium grade. "High purity" with no number is not an answer.

5. Can I see the HPLC chromatogram and the mass spec data?

Why it matters: The chromatogram should show one dominant peak; the mass spec should show the expected and observed masses matching. These are the proof behind the purity and identity claims.

6. Can the COA be matched to my specific batch - and verified?

Why it matters: A COA is only meaningful if it applies to your exact lot and is genuine. Ideally it can be confirmed on the testing lab's own system, since COA images can be edited.

7. How recent is the testing on the batch I will receive?

Why it matters: An old testing date often means the supplier reuses one result instead of testing each batch. Current, batch-level testing is the standard.

8. How do you store and ship your products?

Why it matters: Lyophilized peptides are stable for 12-24+ months at -20°C but only 1-6 months at room temperature. Proper cold storage and protective, insulated packaging determine what condition product arrives in.

9. What are your shipping, storage, and return policies?

Why it matters: Clear, published policies signal an accountable business. Vague or missing policies signal the opposite.

10. How do you ensure consistency between batches?

Why it matters: Quality control is what keeps order #1 and order #10 the same. A serious supplier can explain how its process and testing maintain consistency.

How to use these questions

You do not need a perfect score on all ten, but you want clear, confident, specific answers - especially on testing, mass spec identity, and documentation (questions 1-7). Deflection, vagueness, or irritation at being asked are themselves answers.

Frequently asked questions
